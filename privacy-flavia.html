Privacy Policy

Last updated: 22 April 2026

Flavia is a closed research study tool for a small group of participants tracking eczema triggers. Your data is stored on your device, encrypted in backup, and never sold or shared with advertisers. This policy explains exactly what we collect and why.

1. Who we are

Flavia is an iOS research study app operated by Skin Intelligence, founded by Olivia Vega (London, UK). For this policy the data controller is Olivia Vega, contactable at olivia.delvega@gmail.com.

2. Scope of this policy

This policy covers the Flavia iOS app only. It does not cover the Flavia website at flavialab.com or the Skin Intelligence waitlist. Flavia is distributed via Apple's TestFlight beta program to a limited group of research-study participants and is not available on the public App Store.

3. Data we collect

3.1 Information you provide in the app

  • Scratching events — timestamp, severity (0-5), optional body location.
  • Meal entries — audio recording (processed on-device and not retained), transcribed text, and the structured list of items you confirm.
  • Flare check-ins — daily skin score (0-5), optional photo, optional text notes.

3.2 Data from connected services (with your authorization)

  • Whoop — if you connect your Whoop account, Flavia reads daily metrics: heart-rate variability, resting heart rate, sleep duration, sleep score, recovery score, strain score. Read-only. You can revoke access anytime in your Whoop account settings.
  • Apple Health (HealthKit) — if you grant permission, Flavia reads heart-rate variability, resting heart rate, sleep samples, and active-energy samples from your Apple Watch or iPhone.

3.3 Sensor data for sun-exposure estimation

  • Approximate location — used to look up the current UV index for your area. Coordinates are not stored beyond the daily UV-index lookup.
  • Motion activity — "stationary", "walking", or similar classifications from Core Motion, used to estimate time spent outdoors. Raw motion data is not retained.

3.4 Device data

  • Your Apple ID (via Sign in with Apple) is used solely to identify your study device. We receive an anonymized Apple identifier, not your email or name, unless you choose to share them.

4. What we do not collect

  • We do not collect your contacts, photos library (beyond the single photo you may attach to a check-in), SMS messages, calendar, or browsing history.
  • We do not run third-party analytics SDKs (no Google Analytics, Firebase Analytics, Facebook SDK, Mixpanel, Segment, etc.).
  • We do not run third-party advertising SDKs.
  • We do not sell your data. Ever.

5. How your data is stored

  • On your iPhone — all data is written to local encrypted storage managed by the Stanford Spezi framework. Data stays on your device unless you choose to export or back up.
  • Encrypted cloud backup — if you enable it, your data is backed up to your personal iCloud with end-to-end encryption. Only you hold the keys.
  • Voice recordings — transcribed on-device via Apple's speech recognition. The audio file is deleted immediately after transcription; only the text transcript is kept.
  • Whoop tokens — stored in the iOS Keychain, never transmitted except to Whoop's own servers during the standard OAuth refresh flow.

6. How your data is used

  • To show you your own logs and trends inside the app.
  • To produce a CSV export, on your explicit request, that you can share with the research team running the study.
  • No other use. We do not train machine-learning models on your data without separate, explicit consent.

7. Sharing with third parties

Flavia shares your data with no third parties by default. The only outbound data flows are:

  • Whoop — standard OAuth requests to api.prod.whoop.com to authenticate and fetch your own Whoop data. Governed by Whoop's own privacy policy.
  • UV index provider — an anonymous lookup to a weather API (Open-Meteo or equivalent) using your current approximate coordinates to return the UV index. No account, no tracking identifier attached.
  • Apple TestFlight — crash reports generated by iOS may be shared with the developer via Apple's standard TestFlight telemetry. No user content is included.

If you choose to export your data and send the resulting CSV file to someone (for example, the research team), that is your action and not a data flow initiated by the app.

8. Your rights

  • Access and export — tap "Export my data" in Settings to generate a CSV of everything Flavia has about you.
  • Deletion — uninstalling the app deletes all local data. Request confirmation of deletion by emailing olivia.delvega@gmail.com.
  • Correction — edit any meal, scratch, or check-in entry directly in the app.
  • Withdrawal — you can leave the study at any time. Email us and we will remove any data you have shared with the research team.

If you are a resident of the UK, EEA, or California, you also have rights under the UK GDPR, EU GDPR, and CCPA respectively, including the right to lodge a complaint with your local data-protection authority.

9. Children

Flavia is not directed at children under 16 and the study does not enroll minors.

10. Security

Flavia relies on iOS platform security: Keychain for secrets, Data Protection encryption for files, App Sandbox isolation, and HTTPS for all network calls. No system is perfectly secure, but we do not transmit your data to any Flavia-operated server in this version of the app.

11. Medical disclaimer

Flavia is a research study tool. It is not a medical device, not a diagnostic, and not a substitute for professional medical advice. If you have a health concern, consult a clinician.

12. Changes to this policy

If we materially change how data is handled, we will update this page and notify study participants in-app before the change takes effect.

13. Contact

Questions or requests: olivia.delvega@gmail.com.
